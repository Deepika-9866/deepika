Education Hub

Biodegradable Waste (Green Bin)

This category is for organic waste that decomposes naturally. Proper disposal is vital for creating nutrient-rich compost.

  • Examples: Fruit/vegetable peels, leftover food, coffee grounds, tea bags, eggshells, garden trimmings.
  • Action: Use for home composting or place in your designated municipal green bin.

Recyclable Waste (Blue Bin)

These items can be processed and remanufactured into new products, saving energy and natural resources.

  • Examples: Plastic bottles (PET #1), milk jugs (HDPE #2), paper, cardboard, metal cans, glass containers.
  • Action: Always rinse containers to remove food residue. Check local guidelines for specific plastic types accepted.

Hazardous Waste (Red Bin)

This waste is potentially harmful to humans and the environment and must be handled with extreme care.

  • Examples: Batteries, electronics (e-waste), fluorescent light bulbs, expired medicines, paint cans, pesticides.
  • Action: Never throw in regular trash. Find designated local drop-off points for safe disposal.
